PCR Testing at Boynton Health

What is a PCR Test?

A PCR (polymerase chain reaction) test is a lab technique that our healthcare providers at Boynton use to test for infectious or genetic diseases. You may have had a PCR test done in the past if you received a nasal swab test for COVID-19 or influenza.

How does a PCR test work?

After taking a swab from a body site (such as deep in your nose), our doctors at Boynton will use PCR to make many copies of the DNA (known as amplification) that was in the original sample. This amplification allows your doctor to better analyze the DNA and identify a very specific sequence of molecules that's unique to the DNA it's looking for (target DNA).

For example, your doctor could find a sequence of molecules that could be the genetic code of a virus or bacteria. In testing for infectious diseases, like viruses, the solution has labels that allow the doctor to see whether the sample is positive or negative for the target DNA.

Getting Tested: What does it look like?

The lab technician will take a sample of mucus from (a sample from far back in your nasal cavity) to look for common infections like influenza (the flu), COVID-19, or respiratory syncytial virus (RSV). See figure 1 for how this may look.

Boynton will notify you ahead of time if there is anything you must do to prepare for the test, but most often there is no preparation needed to take a PCR test.

Results and Follow-Up

The results of nasal swab tests for common viruses like the flu and COVID-19 usually take about a day to come back.

A positive PCR test ("detected") results means that it is likely that you have the virus that you were tested for, or you recently recovered from it. You should plan to miss classes and other in-person activities for the next few days to limit the spread of the virus.

A negative PCR test ("not detected") means you probably don't have a virus at the time of your nasal swab. You may still have an infection or virus, but you were tested too early and it wasn't detectable at the time of the PCR test. If you still have symptoms a few days after getting a PCR test you should consider coming back into Boynton and getting tested again.
